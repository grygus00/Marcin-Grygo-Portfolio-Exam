This module introduced the foundations of web development and digital design. Across three assignments, I created stylized one-page websites to explore the use of semantic HTML, CSS layout, and basic visual theory. The projects drew inspiration from themes such as Russian Constructivism, early computing history, and green technology, offering a mix of historical, technical, and sustainability-focused storytelling through web interfaces.
2022 – 1st Semester

This module focused on foundational UX principles, emphasizing research-driven design and user-centered thinking. I explored methods such as user journey mapping, persona creation, and wireframing to develop solutions tailored to real user needs. The final project involved designing a virtual fitting room experience for an e-commerce platform, combining functionality with intuitive navigation.
2023 – 2nd Semester

This module focused on interaction logic, animation principles, and web-based game development. I designed and programmed a browser game titled Wild Hunt, applying CSS animations and JavaScript for dynamic behavior and user interaction. The project challenged me to think structurally and logically while maintaining playful aesthetics and intuitive gameplay.
2023 – 3nd Semester

This group-based module extended our design process into real-world collaboration with an external client. The project involved rebranding and redesigning a company’s digital presence, including a refreshed visual identity, a new website layout, and the creation of promotional video content for social media. Our role required strong communication, strategic research, and hands-on content production to meet the client’s needs across multiple platforms.
2024 – 5th Semester
